The problem

Healthcare in Nigeria is reactive.
We're changing that.

You see a doctor when you're sick. But the conditions that kill most Africans — diabetes, hypertension, pregnancy complications — build silently over months. Until now.

💊

Medication non-adherence

Studies show over 60% of patients with chronic conditions in Nigeria miss doses regularly — because there's no daily reminder or accountability system.

🤰

Maternal mortality

Nigeria accounts for 20% of global maternal deaths. Most are preventable. But without ANC tracking tools and education, risks go undetected between visits.

📱

No affordable daily care

Telehealth startups charge per consultation. Most Nigerians can't afford ₦5,000–₦15,000 per call. There is no affordable, always-on preventive layer.

🏥

Overwhelmed clinics

Doctors spend 70% of consultations on information you could have gotten from a trusted digital companion. AFYA frees that time for what only doctors can do.
