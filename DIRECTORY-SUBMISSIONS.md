AI-powered health companion for managing diabetes, hypertension, and pregnancy. Offline-capable PWA with Claude-powered AI chat, medication tracking, and vitals logging. ₦2,500/month premium.

### Long Description
AFYA is an offline-first Progressive Web App that brings structured, AI-supported health management to Nigerians managing chronic conditions without reliable medical oversight.

**The Gap AFYA Fills:**

Millions of Nigerians live with diabetes, hypertension, pregnancy complications, and other chronic conditions that require consistent daily management. Yet the reality is fragmented: clinic visits happen once or twice yearly. Medication reminders come from family memory. Vitals are logged nowhere. There's no systematic way to understand your own health patterns or get real-time support when something feels off.

Existing health apps don't work here. They assume unlimited mobile data, consistent electricity, and integration with Western healthcare systems. AFYA is built for the actual constraints of Nigeria.

**How AFYA Works:**

Users download AFYA as a Progressive Web App (no app store needed — works through any browser). It operates offline. When connected, it syncs all data securely.

The core interaction is AI-powered health chat. Instead of generic FAQ bots, AFYA uses Claude to have contextual conversations: "I forgot my medication yesterday — should I take it today?" "My blood pressure was high this morning — what should I do?" "I'm experiencing nausea since starting this medication." These aren't symptom checkers that scare you into running to a hospital. They're supportive conversations that help you understand your own condition and make informed decisions.

Supporting this are structured tracking tools: medication adherence logging (did you take it? when? any issues?), vitals entry (blood pressure, blood sugar, weight), and trend visualization. The premium feature — a weekly health digest — synthesizes a week's data into actionable insights: "Your BP has been consistently elevated on mornings after stress; consider evening walks."

**For whom:**

Primary users are Nigerian adults (18-65+) managing conditions long-term. These are people who know their medication regimen, understand their condition, but lack consistent medical oversight. Secondary users include family members supporting a patient's management.

**Pricing Model:**

Free tier: 3 AI chats per day, unlimited medication tracking, vitals logging, push reminders. Genuinely useful for someone who knows what they're doing.

Premium: ₦2,500/month (~$1.50 USD). Unlocks unlimited AI chats and weekly health digests. Pricing is intentional — ₦2,500 is what one round of quality medication costs. It's affordable for the market; it's sustainable for the product.

**Technical Foundation:**

Built as a Progressive Web App specifically because Nigerian internet is unpredictable. Works on poor connections. Works offline. Syncs automatically when reconnected. No app store dependency. Encryption built in from the start.

AFYA is not trying to replace doctors or clinics. It's filling the massive gap between clinic visits — providing structure, reminders, and support that users currently don't have.

AFYA: I Built an AI Health Companion for Diabetes Management in Nigeria (offline-first, ₦2,500/month)

### Post Body

Hey IH — sharing AFYA, which launched this week.

**The Story:**

My mum has diabetes. She's managed it for 15+ years. What struck me watching her was how little support existed for the actual daily management. Doctor visits happen once yearly. Medication reminders? Family. Tracking whether things are improving? Nowhere. Just isolated knowledge and hope.

I started asking: What if Nigerians managing chronic conditions had structured support designed for their actual reality, not some Silicon Valley fantasy of healthcare?

AFYA started there.

**What It Is:**

An offline-first Progressive Web App that combines AI health chat (Claude-powered), medication management, vitals tracking, and weekly health insights. Built specifically for managing diabetes, hypertension, and pregnancy in Nigeria.

**Why Offline-First Matters:**

Nigerian internet is unpredictable. Data can be expensive. Many users are in areas with 2G. So I built AFYA as a PWA that works completely offline. Your medications, vitals, chat history — all accessible. Everything syncs automatically when you reconnect.

Most apps ignore this. It was non-negotiable for me.

**Why Claude AI:**

Because medication management isn't a checklist problem. People struggle with adherence for real reasons: side effects, cost, forgetfulness, skepticism, life circumstances. Generic chatbots don't help. Claude-powered conversations can actually listen and respond contextually.

**Business Model:**

Free: 3 AI chats/day, unlimited medication tracking, vitals logging, reminders. Genuinely useful.

Premium: ₦2,500/month (~$1.50 USD) for unlimited AI chats and weekly health digests. I deliberately priced this at what one round of quality medication costs — it's affordable for Nigeria and sustainable for me.

**Early Metrics:**

~40 beta users across Lagos, Abuja, Port Harcourt. Retention is strong. People engage multiple times daily. The feedback has been: "I feel less alone managing this" and "I actually understand my own patterns now."

**What I've Learned:**

1. **Market validation is different for Nigeria.** I didn't do customer discovery in Figma. I built with real people managing real conditions and iterated based on actual usage.

2. **Pricing matters culturally.** Pricing based on markets, not standard SaaS norms, changes who you serve. ₦2,500/month is accessible; $10/month would exclude people.

3. **Constraints are features.** Building offline-first because internet sucks in Nigeria isn't a compromise — it's the actual value proposition. It's what makes the product useful.

4. **AI changes the interaction model.** Before Claude, I was building a tracking app with generic support. AI meant I could build a companion. Conversations replace checklists.

5. **Founder-market fit matters.** I'm solving a problem I personally watched happen. That clarity has shaped every decision.

**What's Next:**

Expanding condition coverage (more pregnancy support, general wellness). Exploring partnerships with clinics and pharmacies in Nigeria. Testing whether a version of this works in other African countries with similar constraints.

You can try it: https://getafya.co

Happy to discuss the architecture, business decisions, or anything else.
